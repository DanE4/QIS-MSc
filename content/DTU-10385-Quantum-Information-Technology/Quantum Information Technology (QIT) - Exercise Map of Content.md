## 01. Quantum Theory Background (Chapter 2)
*   [[Measurements and Observables]]:
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 23-25):** Prob. 2.5 (Pauli measurement probabilities), Prob. 2.10 (Positivity of POVM elements), Prob. 2.12 (Minimum-error state discrimination), Prob. 2.13 (Unambiguous state discrimination).
*   [[Qubits]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 23-24):** Prob. 2.1-2.3 (Bloch sphere mapping and density matrices), Prob. 2.4, 2.6 (Pauli operators), Prob. 2.8 (Bloch sphere rotations), Prob. 2.9 (Purity of states).
*   [[Entanglement and Density Operators]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 23-24):** Prob. 2.7 (Partial trace and entanglement), Prob. 2.11 (Positivity of reduced states).

## 02. Quantum Light (Chapter 3)
*   [[The Quantum Harmonic Oscillator]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (p. 68):** Prob. 3.1, 3.2 (Commutation relations and rotated quadratures).
*   [[Phase Space]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (p. 68):** Prob. 3.4 (Reality of the Wigner function), Prob. 3.5 (Wigner function marginals).
*   [[Advanced Quantum States]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (p. 68):** Prob. 3.6 (Single-mode squeezing transformation), Prob. 3.7 (Two-mode squeezing transformation).
    *   **Exams:** Reexam F24, Problem 1: "A squeezed state of light" (Covers Heisenberg uncertainty relations and two-mode squeezing).
*   [[The Optical Toolbox]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 68-69):** Prob. 3.8 (Beam splitters between coherent states), Prob. 3.9 (Implementing displacements), Prob. 3.10 (Coherent states subject to loss), Prob. 3.11 (Quadratures and beam splitters).
    *   **Exams:** Exam F24, Problem 1: "Fock states in a beam-splitter network" (Covers output states, photon number measurement, and conditional states).
*   [[Measuring Light]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (p. 69):** Prob. 3.12 (Squeezing and loss in homodyne detection), Prob. 3.13 (Lossy bucket detector POVM).

## 03. Quantum Sensing (Chapters 4-6)
*   [[Parameter Estimation]] & [[Quantum Fisher Information]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 84-85):** Prob. 4.1 (Vanishing mean score), Prob. 4.2 (Additivity of Fisher information), Prob. 4.3 (MSE, variance, and bias), Prob. 4.5 (QFI for pure states), Prob. 4.6 (SQL and Heisenberg scaling for pure states).
    *   **Exams:** Exam F24, Problem 2: "Sensing a two-qubit interaction" (Covers QFI of unitary encodings and optimal input states/measurements).
*   [[Sensing with Light]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 84, 87-89):** Prob. 4.4 (NOON-state interferometry), Prob. 5.1 (Coherent and squeezed-state interferometry), Prob. 5.2 (QFI for NOON states), Prob. 5.3 (Photon loss in interferometry).
    *   **Exams:** Reexam F24, Problem 2: "Phase estimation with quadrature measurements" (Covers error propagation and classical vs. quantum Fisher Information for coherent states).
*   [[Sensing with Qubits]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 91-92):** Prob. 6.1 (Ramsey measurement and optimal time), Prob. 6.2 (Rabi measurement with non-zero free Hamiltonian), Prob. 6.3 (NV thermal populations).
    *   **Exams:** Exam F25, Problem 1: "Qubit thermometry" (Covers thermal equilibrium states, symmetric logarithmic derivatives, and QFI for temperature estimation).

## 04. Quantum Communication (Chapters 8-10)
*   [[Quantum Randomness Certification]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 97-101):** Prob. 8.1 (Properties of entropies), Prob. 8.2 (Estimating extractable randomness), Prob. 8.3 (White-hat Eve game), Prob. 8.4 (Toeplitz hashing), Prob. 8.5 (CHSH inequality and CHSH game), Prob. 8.6 (Quantum CHSH correlations), Prob. 8.7 (Trace distance).
    *   **Exams:** Exam F24, Problem 3: "QRNG by homodyning" (Covers measurement discretization/binning, min-entropy, and adversary strategies). Exam F25, Problem 2: "Beam splitting and randomness" (Covers eavesdropper guessing probability and min-entropy with beam splitters).
*   [[Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)]]: 
    *   **Lecture Notes (pp. 103-104):** Prob. 9.1 (Perfect secrecy), Prob. 9.2 (Six-state BB84 protocol), Prob. 9.3 (Coherent-state prepare-and-measure schemes and mutual information).
    *   **Exams:** Reexam F24, Problem 3: "Qutrit QKD" (Covers orthonormal qutrit bases, min-entropy of trits, and intercept-resend attack error rates).

## 05. Quantum Computing (Chapter 7)
*   [[Measurement-Based and Continuous Variable Computing]]:
    *   **Exams:** Exam F25, Problem 3: "Dual-rail photonic qubits" (Covers physical realizations of dual-rail qubits, single-qubit unitaries, and the Knill–Laflamme–Milburn (KLM) scheme for probabilistic implementation of nonlinear gates).
